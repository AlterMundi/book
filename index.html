About the book

The ratio as the minimal unit of information

The ratio, not the absolute frequency

The proportional relationship between frequencies is the minimal carrier of information. HIT investigates whether harmonic structures are computationally learnable, cross-modal, and biologically meaningful.

Physical · Biological · Affective · Symbolic

The book embraces inconsistency as productive, resisting reduction to a forced unity. Six central hypotheses (H1–H6) articulate the experimental program across distinct domains of validity.

The ecosystem

Theory in practice

Artefact
Harmonic Beacon

"Making the interference pattern of the subtle audible in the material realm."

An electromagnetic kalimba exciter that implements the natural harmonic series F×1 through F×5 as physical vibration — and a companion app that makes those vibrations continuously accessible. The Beacon is both experimental instrument and sonic artwork: a 24/7 live stream of natural harmonics, with audio overlay sessions and biometric validation protocols designed to test the affective and neurological implications of harmonic structures.

harmonicbeacon.com →
Research instrument
Phideus

"Do frequency ratios constitute a universal informational language?"

An AI research program testing whether harmonic frequency ratios transfer across sensory modalities. Using VICReg contrastive learning on audio↔MIDI cross-modal retrieval, Phideus asks whether shared ratio structure between domains enables cross-modal recognition — independently of timbre, instrument, or absolute pitch. Cross-modality is the testbed, not the objective. Named after Phidias, the Greek sculptor whose mastery of proportion defined classical form.

Authorship

The authors

Mariano Fernández Méndez
Author

Mariano Fernández Méndez is an author of the book, a researcher, and a musician. A member of Asociación Civil AlterMundi, he was responsible for the final compilation, writing, and conceptual architecture of the work. His research moves across information theory, acoustics, and transdisciplinary epistemologies.

His work begins with a central question: if proportional relationships between frequencies constitute a level of information organization prior to any cultural encoding, what are the implications for biology, computation, and subjective experience?

Nicolás Echaniz
Author

Nicolás Echaniz is an author of the book, co-founder of Asociación Civil AlterMundi, and a researcher in community networks, free software, and sovereign technologies. He leads the conceptual and technical direction of the HIT project and created the harmonic Beacon, the electromagnetic kalimba exciter that brings the theory's hypotheses into the realm of physical vibration.

He is the author of the founding premise: "In the beginning, there was one thing. The thing stretched. And as it stretched, it wiggled." This formulation captures the core of HIT: the passage from unity to movement as the generative act of all information. His work brings together musicology, technology studies, and artificial intelligence to explore the harmonic structure of sound and meaning.
